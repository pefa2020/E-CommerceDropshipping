Details

Grab these wipes before your next hike or picnic. Or if you live in a particularly mosquito-dense place and just like to go outside. They're a lot easier to use than a spray, and there's no more worrying about the breeze blowing repellent back into your face. They go on like an oil and work for up to 4 hours. And since plants naturally need to defend against insects to survive, it's no wonder these plant-based wipes work

Ingredients

How to Use

This goes on more like a dry body oil than a water-based spray. Apply and gently rub into skin.

Recycling

The outer box (for the 10 count) and the plastic container (for the 25 count) are both curbside recyclable. The wipes are industrially compostable, and the foil-lined wrappers (10 count) can be recycled through TerraCycle by following our special instructions.

These are general guidelines for broadly-recyclable materials. Check with your local recycler to confirm what they take.

How it does good

Most cleaners are made with overly-aggressive and hard chemicals, which can be toxic and damaging. That's why Aunt Fannie's set out to find a better way. They're committed to creating clean, safe, and effective products. With vinegar, probiotics, and essential oils at the forefront, their ingredients are a mindful cleaning revolution. Take time to honor yourself and your home with healthier products.

Aunt Fannie's

Inspired by the strength of his beloved grandmother and his desire to protect the health of his family, Mat Franken set out on a journey to find a better, safer way to clean our homes. From there, he started Aunt Fannies with the mission to add good to every product and every task. After all, we spend 95% of our lives indoors. Mat learned that not every surface we see needs a surgical-level scrub down, and sometimes, nature does know best. With that in mind, he made sure that every person in every home could surround themselves with something good.
