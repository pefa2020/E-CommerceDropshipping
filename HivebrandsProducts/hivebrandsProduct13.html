Details

Ah, green tea. It has a lower caffeine content than coffee, and tastes less intense than black tea. But this brew still makes a statement all on its own. With aromatic hints of bell pepper and fresh grass, and a pretty green-gold color, it's an exquisite cup that's equal parts refreshing and calming. Each bag can also be brewed two or three times, which is definitely an added bonus.

How it does good

Most branded teas sold in supermarkets are blends of teas from different tea gardens. Teatulia Tea hails from their single garden in the Northern Bangladesh Tetulia region. It means they can control every aspect of how they treat the tea and the land it's grown on, and they can really support the people who cultivate it. And since their tea comes direct from their garden to your cup, Teatulia can keep their tea tasting fresh.

Third Party Certifications

Teatulia

Teatulia's garden-to-cup teas from Bangladesh not only taste good, they do good, too. For the land, for the people, and for the environment. Their garden started as barren land that they brought back to life through regenerative farming. Women working in their garden benefit from innovative education, and cattle-lending programs that help lift their families out of poverty. And since most of their teas are sourced from this garden, every sip is fresh, chemical-free, and clean.
